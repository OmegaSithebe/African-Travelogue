St Lucia & Hluhluwe

Where the Battle of the Blood echoes through history - A journey into Zulu kingdom heritage, Big 5 safaris, and iSimangaliso's pristine wetlands

Experience the Battlefields & Wetlands

Popular Attractions

  • Battle of the Blood River (Ncome River): Visit the historic battlefields where Zulu and Voortrekker forces clashed in 1838. The Ncome Museum offers powerful insights into this pivotal moment in South African history.
  • Hluhluwe-iMfolozi Game Reserve: The oldest proclaimed reserve in Africa, famous for its successful rhino conservation. See the Big 5, including white and black rhinos, in stunning savannah landscapes.
  • iSimangaliso Wetland Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring diverse ecosystems - from coral reefs to coastal forests, hippo-filled lakes, and sea turtle nesting beaches.
  • St Lucia Estuary: Take a boat cruise to see hippos, crocodiles, and over 500 bird species in Africa's largest estuarine system.
  • Cape Vidal: Snorkel in crystal-clear waters, spot dolphins and whales, and enjoy pristine beaches within the park.
  • Dumazulu Cultural Village: Experience authentic Zulu traditions, dancing, and crafts in a traditional homestead setting.

Cultural & Historical Highlights

The region is steeped in Zulu history and the legacy of King Shaka. The "Battle of the Blood" refers to the fierce conflicts between Zulu warriors and Voortrekkers at the Ncome River in 1838. Today, you can explore monuments, museums, and guided battlefield tours that bring this dramatic history to life.

Beyond the battlefields, Zulu culture thrives through traditional ceremonies, vibrant beadwork, and storytelling. The harmony between nature and heritage makes this region unique - from tracking rhinos on foot to learning about traditional Zulu medicine from local healers.
